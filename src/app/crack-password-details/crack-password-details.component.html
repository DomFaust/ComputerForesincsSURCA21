Welcome to this section on Dictionary Attacks!

This section explains the basics of what dictionary attacks are, and how they work.

Dictionary Attack
A cyber attack in which a preselected library of terms, or dictionary, is used to guess possible passwords. Hackers can choose to use dictionaries that include patterns that victims may have followed when creating their passwords. Once the password is successfully acquired, all of the victim's information is now made accessible. Brute Force
The process of guessing possible combinations for passwords, encryption keys, or login credentials using only trial-and-error. Once the password is acquired, it is then utilized to hack into a victim's account(s) and obtain access to their personal information. Wordlist
Also known as a dictionary, it is a structure consisting of numerous terms that a password cracking tool utilizes to crack the password and break into a victim's account
